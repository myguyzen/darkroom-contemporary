Not a donation.
Fuel for a process.

The Institute of Curiosity is where Darkroom develops new work, builds its ensemble, and makes space for emerging artists. Public funding takes us part of the way. The rest comes from people who believe the work matters.

Every contribution goes directly into the lab: studio time, artist fees, development programmes, community access. A contribution here is not a transaction with an organisation. It is fuel poured into a specific, living process.

What the lab runs on

Studio time
Floor space and hours for making new work
The Bree Street studio is where Darkroom makes work. Keeping it available for long-form development — not just classes and events — takes resources beyond what ticket sales cover.
Artist fees
Paying the ensemble in development, not just in performance
Darkroom's ensemble works across the full arc of a project. Fees paid in development — not only at performance — are the difference between making the work properly and making it under pressure.
Emerging artists
Screen Dance Lab, Unfinished showings, mentorship
The Institute's programmes — Screen Dance Lab, Unfinished, residency time — exist for artists who are ready for serious development but not yet ready for the main stage. These cost more than they earn.
Community access
Keeping the studio open to the city
Free Studio Sessions and subsidised class rates mean the work is not just for people who can afford a full ticket. Community access is a line item, not an afterthought.
