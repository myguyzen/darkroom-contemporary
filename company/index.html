About the company

"We are building the worlds we want to see: meaningful work for artists, art that speaks to our time, and spaces that challenge the limits of what is possible."

Darkroom Contemporary is a Cape Town–based contemporary performance creation company, founded in 2010 by Louise Coetzer and Oscar O'Ryan. The company makes live performance, dance film and screen dance — work created to tour, and work created to last.

The practice sits at the intersection of choreography and new technology. Over fifteen years, Darkroom has integrated kinetic motion sensing, full dome projection, interactive projection mapping, telematic performance, artificial intelligence and experimental sound into its live and screen-based works — not as novelty, but as artistic language. Every integration serves the choreography.

The work has been presented at Ars Electronica (Linz), Cinedans (Amsterdam), POOL Movement Art Film Festival (Berlin), the South African State Theatre, Zeits MOCAA, Norval Foundation, the British Film Institute (London), and Third Coast Dance Film Festival (Texas). Screen dance film Blue Funk was the first South African work selected for Cinedans, winning Best of Festival at Third Coast 2024 and Best Female Director at Mirada Corta 2025 (Mexico City).

Darkroom is a registered Non-Profit Organisation and Public Benefit Organisation. In June 2026, the company opened a permanent studio on Bree Street, Cape Town — home of the Institute of Curiosity, its platform for movement practice, emerging artists, public discourse and screen dance.

Founders

Louise Coetzer
Executive Artistic Director · Choreographer

Louise Coetzer is a multidisciplinary dance artist working in choreography, site-specific performance, screendance, and digital and tech-based art. Her works grapple with the tensions between the physical and psychological — blending dance theatre with immersive installation, combining distinct physicality with intricately woven technological elements.

Her screen dance film Blue Funk was the first South African work selected for Cinedans (Netherlands), winning Best of Festival at Third Coast Dance Film Festival 2024 (Texas) and Best Female Director at Mirada Corta 2025 (Mexico City). Recent works include autoplay, South Africa's first AI opera; ULTRA for the South African State Theatre; and blame it on the algorithm, commissioned for the Woordfees and the National Arts Festival. She received the Artist Award 2022 at POOL Movement Art Festival (Berlin) and Most Innovative Contribution to Dance at the DCAS Cultural Affairs Awards 2023. She is a guest lecturer at UCT's Centre for Theatre, Dance and Performance Studies.

Oscar O'Ryan
Co-founder · Cinematographer · Visual Artist

Oscar O'Ryan (b. 1978, South Africa) is a cinematographer, photographer, visual artist and filmmaker. His work spans commissioned film, photo reportage, experimental video art and live production design — a practice held together by a commitment to cinematic precision and the language of the moving image.

As co-founder of Darkroom Contemporary, he merges visual media expertise with choreographic work, resulting in productions that operate equally on stage and screen. His films have screened across festivals internationally. Recent recognition includes the Artist Award 2022 at POOL Movement Art Film Festival (Berlin), Best Film Award at Denton Black Film Awards (USA) and Best International Shorts at Berlin Shorts Film Festival 2024 (Germany).
